Biography

Eun Sohl (Stella) Koh is a research engineer focused on physics-informed ML and automation for semiconductor process. She spent 9+ years at Intel, most recently as a Software Research Engineer in computational lithography, following work as a TD Module Development Engineer in Class 1 cleanroom environments. She received her Ph.D. in Physical Chemistry from Indiana University, advised by Prof. Bogdan Dragnea.

Research engineer with deep roots in both fabrication and computation. At Intel, she developed and optimized BEOL etch processes, then moved into computational lithography — building ML-assisted resist CD models and Python automation frameworks for reticle tapeout that cut turnaround by ~80%. PhD research established a foundation in nanoscale optical instrumentation, including a custom photothermal interference microscope and integrated FEM/FDTD thermo-optics simulation.

Portland, OR (open to Bay Area)
